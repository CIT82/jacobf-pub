The Daybreak canon paints a harrowing picture of a world forever changed by a horrific anomaly – SCP-001, also known as "When Day Breaks." This SCP isn't a singular object, but rather a horrifying transformation of the sun itself. In this canon, sunlight becomes lethal, causing any living organism it touches to melt into a waxy, fleshy monstrosity. This event, often referred to as the "solar singularity," unleashes a global catastrophe. The core theme of Daybreak is survival in a world perpetually cloaked in darkness. Humanity is forced to adapt to an existence devoid of sunlight. Imagine vast underground cities powered by alternative energy sources or specially designed suits that allow for limited forays above ground during the night. Every sunrise becomes a terrifying event to be avoided at all costs. With sunlight being lethal, the once hunted become the hunters. The newly transformed creatures, often referred to as "wendigo" or "melted," pose a constant threat. These waxy figures retain a semblance of their former intelligence and are driven by a singular desire – to consume and convert others under the unforgiving light. Mankind’s struggle becomes one of outlasting and potentially even fighting back against these monstrous creations. The Daybreak canon explores the rise of various factions vying for survival and dominance in this desolate world. The Foundation, its resources stretched thin, focuses on containing the spread of the melted and researching potential solutions. Other groups might emerge, each with their own agendas: some seeking a cure, others exploiting the chaos for personal gain. A sense of fragile hope persists, with some tales exploring the possibility of reversing the effects of SCP-001 or finding a new habitable world.
