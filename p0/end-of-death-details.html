The End of Death canon within the SCP Foundation universe presents a chilling scenario: humanity has achieved a form of immortality, but not in the utopian way one might imagine. In this canon, all living things are fundamentally changed, unable to die from natural causes, disease, or even severe injuries. This "immortality" comes with a hefty price tag, fundamentally altering society and forcing the Foundation to adapt its mission. While physical death is no longer a concern, the human body still ages and accumulates damage. Imagine a world filled with the elderly and terminally ill, trapped in a state of perpetual suffering. The Foundation faces the daunting task of managing a population that can't die naturally. With an ever-growing population and limited resources, the world grapples with issues of food and space scarcity. The Foundation might be forced to develop and implement population control measures, creating a complex ethical dilemma. The concept of death plays a crucial role in giving meaning to life. In a world without it, existential despair and apathy become major societal issues. The Foundation might need to address these psychological effects to maintain some semblance of order. The Foundation's New Priorities: Population Control: Finding a sustainable way to manage a constantly growing population becomes paramount. This could involve researching birth control methods or even exploring controversial population reduction strategies. Terminal State Replacement: Since true death is no longer possible, the Foundation might focus on researching alternative "end states."
