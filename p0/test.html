Introduction to SCP

A basic overview

SCP stands for "Secure, Contain, Protect." It's a fictional universe created by the SCP Foundation, a collaborative writing community that revolves around a large collection of anomalous objects, entities, and phenomena. These anomalies are classified as SCPs and are contained by the Foundation to prevent them from posing a threat to humanity or the world at large. The SCP Foundation maintains a database of these anomalies, each assigned a unique SCP number. Entries in the database typically include a description of the anomaly, containment procedures to prevent it from causing harm, and any additional information or incidents related to the SCP. SCP entries can range from seemingly harmless objects to world-ending entities, and they often come with rich backstories and lore crafted by writers within the SCP community. The SCP Foundation operates covertly, keeping the existence of anomalies hidden from the general public to avoid causing panic or widespread knowledge of the anomalous. The SCP universe has gained significant popularity online due to its collaborative and creative nature. It encompasses a wide range of media, including written stories, audio logs, artwork, video games, and more, all expanding upon the vast and mysterious world of SCPs..
