SCP-049, classified as Euclid, is contained within a Standard Secure Humanoid Containment Cell at Site-19's Research Sector-02. Transporting SCP-049 requires sedation and the use of a Class III Humanoid Restriction Harness, with at least two armed guards monitoring. While generally cooperative, SCP-049 may exhibit aggression, which should be met with caution. Lavender has a calming effect on SCP-049, and it is provided with the corpse of a recently deceased animal every two weeks for study. Human interaction is prohibited following a Containment Committee Order. SCP-049 resembles a medieval plague doctor, with its robes seemingly integrated into its body. Despite this, X-rays reveal a humanoid skeletal structure underneath. It communicates in various languages but prefers English or medieval French. SCP-049 becomes hostile towards individuals it perceives as afflicted by the "Pestilence," often attempting to kill them through direct skin contact. Autopsies on SCP-049's victims are inconclusive. SCP-049 expresses frustration after killings, proceeding to perform crude surgeries on the corpses, resulting in SCP-049-2 instances. SCP-049-2 are reanimated corpses operated on by SCP-049, lacking prior memories or mental functions but displaying basic motor skills. They can become aggressive when provoked or directed by SCP-049. SCP-049 claims to "cure" subjects through these procedures. SCP-049 was discovered during an investigation in Montauban, France, where it was observed engaging with SCP-049-2 instances before willingly entering Foundation custody.
