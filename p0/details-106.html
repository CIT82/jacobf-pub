    SCP-106 is subjected to stringent containment protocols due to its highly dangerous nature. All physical interaction requires approval by a two-thirds vote from O5-Command and must occur in AR-II maximum security sites after evacuating non-essential staff. Personnel are to maintain a distance of at least sixty meters from SCP-106's containment cell, except during breach events. The containment cell itself is a sealed container made of lead-lined steel, encased in forty layers of identical material separated by empty space. Electromagnetic supports suspend the container at least 60cm from any surface. Additionally, sixteen spherical "cells" filled with various fluids and structures serve as secondary containment, equipped with high-intensity lighting systems. Continuous surveillance is maintained over both containment areas. SCP-106, resembling an elderly humanoid in advanced decomposition, exhibits a predatory behavior pattern. It remains motionless for extended periods, waiting to ambush prey. SCP-106 can scale vertical surfaces and suspend itself upside down indefinitely. When attacking, it targets prey between the ages of 10 and 25, incapacitating them before pulling them into its "pocket dimension." Physical contact with SCP-106 triggers a corrosive effect, causing rapid breakdown and deterioration of materials, including living tissues. This effect lasts for six hours after contact. SCP-106 can pass through solid matter and enter its pocket dimension, accessible from any connected point. The dimension consists of halls and rooms, with limited observation due to its hazardous nature. Observations suggest that SCP-106 may experience confusion in complex environments and aversion to sudden light, reducing escape incidents by 43%. However, its containment remains a priority, with no recovery attempts authorized for lost personnel or objects. Ongoing research aims to further understand and mitigate SCP-106's threat.
