The region of the Bay of Bengal, approximately 300km in diameter, containing SCP-3000 is to be routinely patrolled by Foundation naval vessels to prevent civilian deep-sea exploration. Any individuals believed to have contacted SCP-3000 are to be quarantined and processed at Site-151 indefinitely. Foundation submarine SCPF Eremita is tasked with monitoring SCP-3000's foremost section within the Ganges Fan and executing the Atzak Protocol. The Atzak Protocol mandates strict containment measures aboard the Eremita, prohibiting personnel from leaving the vessel except for protocol-related tasks. Individuals who breach protocol are considered lost. Furthermore, all interactions with SCP-3000 require prior authorization to prevent exposure to its cognitohazardous effects. Description: SCP-3000 is a massive aquatic entity resembling a giant moray eel, with an estimated length between 600 and 900 kilometers. The head of SCP-3000 measures roughly 2.5m in diameter, and sections of the body proper reach up to 10m in diameter. SCP-3000 is primarily sedentary, residing in and around the Ganges Fan. It is carnivorous and excretes a dark grey substance known as Y-909 through its skin during feeding. SCP-3000 is classified as a Class VIII cognitohazardous entity. Direct observation induces severe mental alterations, including head pain, paranoia, fear, panic, and memory loss. Dr. Eugene Getts documented initial encounters with SCP-3000, describing psychological distress among crew members and one member's fatal descent into madness. Discovery: SCP-3000 was discovered in the Bay of Bengal and promptly quarantined by the Foundation to prevent civilian interaction. Ongoing research aims to understand SCP-3000's nature and mitigate its anomalous effects. Efforts include studying SCP-3000's behavior, composition, and the Y-909 substance it excretes during feeding.
