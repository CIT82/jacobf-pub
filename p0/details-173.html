SCP-173 is contained within a securely locked container at all times, necessitating the presence of at least three personnel for entry. Once inside, these individuals must maintain direct eye contact with SCP-173 at all times and ensure that the door is relocked upon exiting. This precautionary measure is crucial, as SCP-173 is known to be animate and exceedingly hostile. Constructed from concrete and reinforced with rebar, SCP-173 also bears traces of Krylon brand spray paint, indicating potential human involvement in its creation. Notably, SCP-173 exhibits immobility when directly observed by personnel; however, any break in eye contact with the object poses a significant risk. To mitigate this danger, personnel entering SCP-173's containment must remain vigilant and alert one another before blinking, thereby preventing any lapse in observation. The aggressive nature of SCP-173 manifests in its attack methods, which include swiftly snapping the neck of its victims or engaging in strangulation. Such incidents require immediate response using Class 4 hazardous object containment procedures to minimize casualties and prevent breaches. It is worth noting that personnel within SCP-173's containment area have reported hearing scraping sounds emanating from the object's vicinity, despite no visible source being present. While this phenomenon is considered a normal occurrence, any deviations from the established pattern should be promptly reported to the designated supervisory personnel. Another peculiar aspect of SCP-173's containment is the presence of a reddish-brown substance on the floor of its enclosure, believed to be a mixture of feces and blood.
